Spaghetti carbonara with red onion

  • Time: 20 minutes
  • Serves: 2
Ingredients Quantity
Spaghetti 250 g
Butter 20 g
Garlic 2
Red Onion 1 head
Bacon 50 g
Cream 20% 200 ml
Grated Parmesan cheese 50 g
Chicken egg 4 pieces
Salt to taste
Ground black pepper to taste

Step 1: Boil water in a large saucepan and cook the pasta until al dente. Usually you need to cook it for a minute less than indicated on the pack.

Step 2: While the pasta is cooking, melt the butter in a frying pan and fry the finely chopped onion, garlic and bacon on it. To softness and to a distinct garlic and fried bacon smell.

Step 3: Remove the pan from the heat and in a deep bowl, whisk four egg yolks with cream and grated parmesan. Add salt and pepper to the mixture, whisk again.

Step 4: In the finished spaghetti, pour the bacon pieces fried with onion and garlic. Pour in the mixture of cream, yolks and parmesan, mix. And serve immediately, sprinkled with freshly grated cheese and ground black pepper.

Brownie

  • Time: 40 minutes
  • Serves: 6
Ingredients Quantity
Dark chocolate 100 g
Butter 180 g
Brown sugar 200 g
Chicken egg 4 pieces
Wheat flour 100 g
Walnuts 100 g

Step 1: Break the chocolate into pieces and melt it together with the butter in a water bath, stirring all the time with a spatula or a wooden spoon. Remove the resulting thick chocolate sauce from the water bath and leave to cool.

Step 2: Meanwhile, mix the eggs with a hundred grams of brown sugar: break the eggs into a separate bowl and beat, gradually adding sugar. You can beat it with a mixer or manually — as you like — but not less than two and a half to three minutes.

Step 3: Chop walnuts on a cutting board with a sharp knife. They can be pre-fried in a dry frying pan until the aroma appears, but this is an optional option.

Step 4: In the cooled melted chocolate with butter, carefully add the remaining sugar, then flour and chopped nuts and mix everything well with a whisk.

Step 5: Preheat the oven to 200 degrees. The bottom of a small deep refractory mold is lined with a sheet of baking paper or tracing paper. Pour the dough into the mold. Put in the oven and bake for twenty—five to thirty minutes until a sugar crust appears.

Step 6: Take the finished pie out of the oven, let it cool and cut into squares with a sharp knife or a pizza knife — so the pieces will turn out especially smooth.

Сottage cheese cheesecakes

  • Time: 30 minutes
  • Serves: 10
Ingredients Quantity
Cottage cheese 350 g
Chicken egg 2 pieces
Wheat flour 6 tablespoons
Sugar 2 tablespoons
Sunflower oil 5 tablespoons

Step 1: Put all the cottage cheese in a saucepan and mash it with a fork so that there are no large lumps left in it. Break the eggs into it, add sugar and mix everything thoroughly. It is better not to use too dry or too wet cottage cheese, otherwise the cheesecakes will fall apart during cooking.

Step 2: Pour 5 tablespoons (with a slide) of flour into the cottage cheese and mix thoroughly. You can add a little more flour, the cheesecakes will then turn out more dense. Or you can add a little less flour, and then the cheesecakes will be more tender. As a result, you should get a homogeneous mass from which you can make cheesecakes.

Step 3: Put the pan on medium heat and pour sunflower oil into it.

Step 4: Pour a little flour on a plate. Make a few small balls of the resulting curd mass and put them on a plate. It is better to sculpt 4-5 balls at once — as many as will fit into the pan at the same time. Then, in turn, roll the curd balls in flour, flatten them into small cakes (they should not be too thin) and put them in a frying pan.

Step 5: Fry the cheesecakes for 1-2 minutes until golden brown. Then turn them over to the other side and also fry until golden brown.
